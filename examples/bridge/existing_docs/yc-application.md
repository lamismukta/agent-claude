# YC Application — Bridge

## Company name
Bridge

## One-liner
AI-powered research assistant that proactively builds your knowledge — not just when you ask.

## What does your company do?
Bridge is a browser extension that helps people learn and research topics by capturing what they find interesting, generating suggestions for what they're missing, and building structured working documents that grow over time instead of getting regenerated every session.

Unlike ChatGPT or Claude projects, Bridge doesn't wait for you to ask the right question. You add topics to a list while browsing, and Bridge figures out what else you should know — finding connections and gaps you wouldn't have searched for.

## Why did you pick this idea to work on?
I kept losing useful knowledge. Every deep dive I did — whether learning about startups, studying a new subject, or exploring a technical topic — ended up scattered across 40 tabs, three ChatGPT conversations, and a half-finished Google Doc. A week later I couldn't find any of it.

I tried using AI to help, but ChatGPT and Claude are reactive — they only help when I ask, and they regenerate from scratch every time instead of building on what's already there. I want something that accumulates knowledge and proactively fills gaps, like a research partner who's always reading ahead.

## What's your progress / traction?
- Built a rough Chrome extension prototype (capture flow works, suggestion generation is placeholder)
- Tested with 8 friends/colleagues informally — 6 said "I'd use this daily" (taking with a grain of salt)
- No revenue, no waitlist yet
- Main signal: 3 of the 8 testers asked to keep using the prototype even though suggestions were hardcoded

## How will you make money?
Freemium: free for 2 lists with limited suggestions. $12/month for unlimited lists, more suggestions per day, and project features. Users bring their own API key or we bundle API costs into the subscription.

## How many founders and who are they?
Solo founder. Background in product management at a mid-stage startup (3 years). Self-taught developer — built internal tools, comfortable with Python and JavaScript but not a senior engineer. Did a CS degree but went into product instead of engineering.

## What do you know that others don't?
The problem isn't that people can't find information — it's that they can't accumulate it. Every AI tool treats each session as a fresh start. The real value is in the compounding: the more you add, the better Bridge understands what you care about, and the more useful its suggestions become.

Most AI research tools are trying to be a better search engine. Bridge is trying to be a better notebook — one that reads ahead for you.

## What are you most worried about?
1. That the suggestion quality won't be good enough to justify the friction of using an extension — if suggestions are obvious or irrelevant, people will just go back to ChatGPT
2. That "proactive" is hard to get right — too aggressive feels spammy, too passive feels like it's not doing anything
3. That I'm a solo non-technical founder building a browser extension, and the technical complexity might slow me down

## How do users find out about you?
Haven't started distribution yet. Plan: post on HN ("Show HN"), share in learning/PKM communities (r/PKMS, Obsidian forums, Roam Research community), and cold outreach to productivity YouTubers for reviews.

## What's the riskiest assumption?
That AI-generated suggestions will surface content users genuinely didn't know about — not just obvious related topics they could have Googled themselves. If the suggestions aren't surprising and useful, the whole product is just a fancy bookmark manager.
