Started by asking about his current workflow for user research → product.

He does 6-8 user calls per week. Notes in Notion. "The notes are pretty good but I barely look at them again."

**The core pain:**
"The problem isn't writing the ticket. The problem is six weeks later when I'm looking at what shipped and thinking — wait, is this actually what they asked for? And I go back to look at the call notes and I can't find them, or I find them and the ticket had already drifted from what they said, and the code drifted from the ticket. There are three layers of telephone."

He described a specific example: customers kept saying onboarding was confusing. He wrote a ticket for a new onboarding checklist. Engineers shipped it. Churn didn't move. Six months later he did more calls and found out users actually wanted to import their existing data on day 1 — the checklist was irrelevant. "The call notes said that. I just missed it when I wrote the ticket."

**On current tools:**
Uses Notion AI to summarise call notes. "It's fine for a summary but it doesn't tell me anything I couldn't figure out in 2 minutes of reading." He wants something that actively catches gaps, not just summarises.

Tried Dovetail for a month. Cancelled. "Too much overhead tagging everything. I'm one person."

**Time spent:**
Estimates 4-5 hours/week on "translating" — going from call notes to tickets, updating PRDs, reviewing PRs to check if they match specs. "It's the most manual part of the job and it's the part where mistakes happen."

**What he'd want:**
"I want something that reads my call notes and tells me: here's what users actually asked for. Then reads my PRD and tells me: here's what you forgot to include. And then — this is the dream — reads the PR and tells me: this doesn't address what users said."

Would pay: "If it actually worked? $200/month easily. $500 if it integrated with Linear."

**Quote to save:**
"The three handoffs are where everything dies. Call to PRD. PRD to ticket. Ticket to code. Each one loses something."
