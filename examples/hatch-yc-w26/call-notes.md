# Call Notes — Hatch

User interviews and pilot check-ins. Copy to `call_notes/` before running `/sprint`.

---

---
who: Marcus Webb, owner, Webb Accounts (2-person firm, Bristol)
date: 2026-01-08
context: Pilot check-in, 30 min. Marcus is the most engaged pilot user.
---

Marcus has been using Hatch daily for 6 weeks. Does the books for ~40 small business clients.

**What he loves:**
"The Tuesday slog used to take me all day. Now it's 2 hours. I go through the flagged ones, approve the rest in bulk. That's it."

Confidence scores are the key thing. "If it says 94% I just approve it. If it says 71% I look. That threshold works for me."

**The client email problem:**
Unprompted, spent 10 minutes on this. "The thing I actually hate more than categorization is writing the monthly update. Every client gets a slightly different version of the same email — here's your P&L, here's what changed, here's what to watch out for. I've been doing it for 8 years and I still spend 2 hours per client per month on it. If you could draft that from the categories, I'd pay double."

Has 40 clients. 2 hours × 40 = 80 hours/month on client emails alone.

**Concern he raised:**
"What happens when it miscategorizes something and the client notices? I'm the one who looks stupid, not the software."

Wants an audit trail. "I need to be able to show the client: here's every category decision, here's what was manual and what was AI. Cover my arse."

**Would he pay?**
"I'm already using it. Of course I'll pay. What's the price?" When I said £150/month he said "is that per user or per firm?" Per user. "That's fine then, it's just me."

---
who: Sarah Okonkwo, director, Okonkwo & Partners (4-person firm, London)
date: 2026-01-22
context: Pilot check-in + product feedback, 45 min
---

Sarah runs a 4-person firm, mix of small business and individual clients. More cautious than Marcus. Only 2 of her staff are using Hatch.

**Adoption problem:**
"Janet doesn't trust it. She goes through every single transaction even after Hatch categorizes them. So for her it's slower, not faster."

Janet is 52, been doing bookkeeping for 25 years. "She doesn't believe the AI is right until she's checked it herself. Which kind of defeats the purpose."

This is the key tension: the efficiency gain requires trusting the output. Bookkeepers who don't trust it are slower, not faster.

**What would build trust:**
"Show her it's more accurate than she is. Not in a mean way. But if you could show the error rate over time — like, here are 500 transactions, Hatch got 97% right — she might come around."

**On the client email feature:**
"We don't do that." (Surprised — I assumed everyone did.) "Our clients are mostly individuals. They don't want a monthly email, they just want their tax return done."

This is a segment split I hadn't thought about. Individual tax clients vs. small business clients — very different needs.

**Revenue:**
Sarah is their accounting software evaluator. "If you can get Janet on board, we'll pay for all 4 seats. That's the test."

---
who: Tom Fenwick, founder, Fenwick Consulting (sole trader, no accounting firm — does his own books)
date: 2026-02-03
context: Inbound — found Hatch through a LinkedIn post, 20 min call
---

Tom is a management consultant who does his own QuickBooks. Not an accountant. The most enthusiastic call I've had.

**His problem:**
"I spend 3 hours every Sunday trying to remember what I spent money on 3 weeks ago. Half my transactions are just labeled 'card payment.' I'm always guessing."

Hatch solves this immediately — he doesn't need high accuracy thresholds, he needs anything better than manual guessing.

**The surprise:**
Tom is not my target user. He's a sole trader, not an accounting firm. But he's willing to pay more (£30/month individual tier) and there are orders of magnitude more of him than there are accounting firms.

**Should I care about this segment?**
Instinct: no. The volume is attractive but the sales motion is totally different (B2C vs B2B), the support burden is higher, and the feature set diverges quickly. But Tom specifically said "all my founder friends have this same problem." That's a data point.

**His ask:**
"Can I just use it now? I don't care that it's a CLI tool."
