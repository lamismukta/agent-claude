# Call Notes — Arjun Mehta, Hatch design partner
**Date:** 2026-03-05
**Who:** Arjun Mehta, co-founder at Vesper (YC W26). Using Hatch as primary recruiting tool.
**Duration:** 35 mins

---

## What they're using Hatch for

Vesper is hiring their first two engineers. Arjun is running the process himself — no recruiter, no HR. He found Hatch through a YC Slack post. Has made 3 match requests, had 2 intro calls, no offers yet.

"The engineers are good. That's not the problem. The problem is I can't tell if they're serious or just browsing."

---

## The core frustration

He described a match with someone who looked perfect on paper — right background, startup experience, referred by someone he respected. Scheduled the intro call. Showed up enthusiastic. Said they'd be ready to start in 2 months. Then went quiet. No response to follow-up.

"I don't know if they took another offer, got cold feet, or just changed their mind. I have no visibility after the intro."

This came up twice more in the call. Both times unprompted.

---

## What he wants

"I just want to know if someone is actually active. If they logged in last week, if they've responded to other founders. Even just a green dot or a last-seen date. Something."

Also mentioned: he wants to be able to add private notes to a candidate profile. Not share them, just for himself. "I take notes in Notion now. It's annoying."

---

## What's working

"The referral thing is real. If I see that an engineer was referred by someone I know, I pay attention. Cold signups I mostly ignore."

He also likes the equity calculator. "I used it to sanity-check our offer. It's the only thing I've found that explains vesting cliffs in plain English."

---

## Would he pay?

"Yeah, for a placed hire. Probably not for access. If you charged me a monthly fee I'd cancel between hires and resubscribe when I needed someone. You'd have to charge on success or I'd churn."

Success fee: "10–15% of first-year comp feels right. Recruiters charge 20–25%. If you're cheaper and better, that's easy."

---

## His suggestions (unprompted)

- Signal on engineer activity: last active date, response rate
- Private notes on candidates
- Some way to see if a candidate is in multiple pipelines ("am I competing with 10 other founders?")
- Faster first contact: "Right now there's too many steps before I can just send a message"
