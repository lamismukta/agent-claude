# YC Application — Hatch

**Company:** Hatch
**Batch:** YC S26
**Founders:** Sofia Reyes (CEO) & Daniel Park (CTO)

---

## What does your company do?

Hatch is a hiring platform for early-stage startups. We match founders directly with senior engineers who want to join startups — not through recruiters, not through job boards. The engineer discovers the role through their network or our curated feed; we handle the rest: intro, scheduling, offer templates, equity calculator.

Recruiting is broken for early-stage companies. Founders spend 40% of their time on hiring in the first 18 months, and most of that is wasted — on inbound noise, unresponsive recruiters, candidates who interview well but don't want to take the risk. We fix the signal problem: every engineer on Hatch has opted in to startup risk and has been vetted by someone who knows them.

---

## Who are your customers?

Series A or earlier startups (1–30 engineers), hiring their first 3–5 technical hires. Typically the founder is making the hire themselves — no dedicated recruiter, no HR, just a founder trying to close a great engineer while also building the product.

Secondary: senior engineers (7+ years) at big tech who want to go earlier but don't know how to find the right opportunity without going through a recruiter or cold-applying.

---

## What have you built?

- A web app with onboarding flows for both sides (founders and engineers)
- A curation layer: engineers are only listed if referred by another engineer already on the platform
- A match flow: founder marks interest → engineer gets intro request → mutual yes → we surface contact info + send intro email
- A dashboard for founders to track their pipeline
- 22 engineers listed, 7 founders with active hiring pipelines
- 3 hires made (2 confirmed, 1 still negotiating offer)

---

## What's your traction?

- 3 hires placed in 6 weeks since launch
- $0 revenue — current model is free; testing willingness to pay
- NPS from founders: 72 (3 ratings, so grain of salt)
- Engineer referral rate: 64% of signups come from referral (vs. 36% cold signup)
- Repeat usage: 5 of 7 founders have returned to make a second match request after their first
- One design partner (Vesper, a YC W26 company) is using Hatch as their primary recruiting tool

---

## Why now?

The AI coding wave is reshaping who startups need to hire. Three years ago, an early-stage startup needed 6 engineers to ship quickly. Today, 2 strong engineers with good taste and the ability to work with AI tools can match that output. The talent bar went up (they need to be exceptional) and the volume went down (they only need 2, not 6). That makes each hire more consequential — and more worth paying to get right.

At the same time, the supply of engineers who want to leave big tech is at a 5-year high. Post-layoffs, post-RTO, there's genuine appetite. The problem isn't convincing engineers to consider it — it's matching them to the right founder at the right moment.

---

## What's the biggest risk to your plan?

The supply problem. We can get founders to pay — they have budget and urgency. Getting engineers to stay active on the platform is harder. Engineers are passive: they sign up, list themselves, and don't log back in. If we can't keep the supply warm, founders will stop trusting the feed.

The referral mechanic helps, but we haven't solved reactivation. Once an engineer is placed (or decides they're not looking), they go dark. We need either a continuous reason to stay engaged, or a much faster match cycle so the window between signup and placement is shorter.
